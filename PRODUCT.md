# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Active swing traders who trade individual stocks and ETFs. They open this dashboard once each morning before the market opens (or during pre-market) to get a single, trusted read on whether market conditions justify taking risk that day. They already understand how to trade; this tool tells them whether they *should*. Decisions happen fast and the stakes are real.

## Product Purpose

A market quality scoring dashboard that aggregates 30+ indicators across five categories (Volatility, Momentum, Trend, Breadth, Macro) into a single Market Quality Score (0-100) and a clear YES / CAUTION / NO trade decision. Success means a trader can open the app, see the decision and score in under five seconds, and trust it enough to act on it.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Clinical. Authoritative.

The dashboard speaks like a seasoned trader reviewing data, not like a product manager pitching features. Every element earns its place by serving the signal.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: Shadcn defaults, card grids, cream/white backgrounds, metric tile arrays, "modern" rounded corners everywhere. This is a trading tool, not a project management app.
- **News / media finance sites**: MarketWatch, Yahoo Finance — ad-heavy, cluttered editorial layouts, mixed signals, no clear decision architecture.
- **Retail fintech (implicit)**: Robinhood-style gamification, pastel gradients, approachable softness. The user is not a beginner.

## Design Principles

1. **Decision first.** The YES/CAUTION/NO verdict and the score are the product. Every other element exists to support or explain that verdict. Never bury the lead.
2. **Earned density.** Data-dense layouts are a feature, not a flaw — they signal expertise and respect the user's literacy. Density becomes clutter only when hierarchy breaks down.
3. **Signal purity.** Green / amber / red carry semantic weight. Use them exclusively for market signals (bullish, caution, bearish). Decorative color competes with meaning.
4. **Terminal authenticity.** Monospace type, precise grids, crisp borders, minimal rounding. The aesthetic is Bloomberg terminal and TradingView Professional — tools traders already trust.
5. **Stable structure.** Traders read the same layout every pre-market session. The interface should be so consistent that the user's eye goes straight to what changed, not to where things are.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Both light (TradingView Professional) and dark (Bloomberg Terminal) themes must meet this standard independently. Signal colors (green / red / amber) must never be the sole differentiator — pair with labels or icons for color-blind users.
